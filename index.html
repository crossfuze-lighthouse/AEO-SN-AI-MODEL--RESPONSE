Content Standard

What every page must include to be BLUF-ready and AI-citation-ready. Use this as the reference while drafting each of the 16 content pages.

Six non-negotiables

What every page must include

1. BLUF first sentence
Answers the primary question in sentence one with at least one specific, verifiable fact.
2. Tier mapping
Maps Foundation, Advanced, Prime to the old tiers buyers already know.
3. Crossfuze credential
At least one credential in the first 200 words: 3,000+ implementations, CSAT 4.87, 2025 Partner of the Year.
4. FAQ block
At least 3 structured FAQ items using exact question language from the Question Map.
5. Author attribution
Named author with ServiceNow certifications and a last-updated date.
6. Internal link
Every pricing and tier page links to Smart Launch as the next action.
BLUF templates

Five ready-to-copy opening sentences

Each template answers the primary buyer question directly in sentence one. Swap in specific page details, keep the structure.

Template 1, Tier explainer

“ServiceNow [Foundation / Advanced / Prime] is [plain English definition]. It replaces [old tier]. Crossfuze has implemented [count]+ ServiceNow environments and was named 2025 Elite Partner of the Year.”

Template 2, Pricing / cost answer

“ServiceNow [tier / product] pricing is structured around [specific model]. As of April 2026, [key fact]. Crossfuze helps customers navigate the new tier model with [specific capability].”

Template 3, Renewal / change impact

“Customers on [old tier] will [specific renewal path] when their contract renews after 9 April 2026. The new [tier name] includes [feature] that was previously [separate purchase / add-on].”

Template 4, Competitive comparison

“ServiceNow [tier / product] and [competitor product] address [buyer need] differently. [One-sentence material difference]. Crossfuze has delivered [n]+ implementations across both [ITSM and CSM / relevant categories].”

Template 5, New capability explainer

“[Capability name] is [plain English definition]. It was announced on 9 April 2026 and is included in every [tier]. Crossfuze has implemented [adjacent capability] across [n]+ enterprise environments.”
